Identify the different tags used in cozycup-boilerplate

!DOCTYPE html - Declares the document type and version of HTML.
html - The root element of the HTML document.
head - Contains meta-information about the document (such as charset, title, etc.).
meta - Provides metadata for the document.
link - Defines the relationship between the document and an external resource (in this case, a CSS file).
title - Specifies the title of the document (shown in the browser tab).
body - Contains the content of the HTML document.
header - Defines the introductory section, often containing navigation links.
nav - Defines navigation links.
div - A generic container for content (in this case, for the logo).
img - Embeds an image.
ul - Defines an unordered list.
li - Defines a list item.
a - Defines a hyperlink.
section - Defines a section in the document (used for grouping content).
h1 - Defines a top-level heading.
h2 - Defines a second-level heading.
p - Defines a paragraph.
footer - Defines the footer section of the document.

My Favourite Movie

Heneral Luna

This movie is about the Filipino general know as Heneral Antonio luna, how he fought for the Philippines and died by hands of his fellow Filipino

Main Character

Gen. Antonio Luna is the fiery and brilliant commander of the Philippine revolutionary forces during the Philippine-American War. Known for his sharp intellect, military expertise, and fierce patriotism, Luna is both respected and feared by his peers and subordinates. His uncompromising stance on discipline and unity often puts him at odds with other leaders, exposing the internal conflicts within the revolutionary government. Despite his abrasive demeanor, his genuine love for his country and desire for true independence drive his every action.

Apolinario Mabini Known as the "Brains of the Revolution," Apolinario Mabini serves as the political conscience in the film. Paralyzed from the waist down, his physical limitations contrast with his sharp mind and unwavering principles. Mabini observes the internal struggles with a sense of frustration, highlighting the revolution’s moral and ideological conflicts.

President Emilio Aguinaldo President Emilio Aguinaldo is portrayed as a complex figure, torn between his leadership responsibilities and the influence of his close allies. His indecisiveness and political maneuvering contribute to the growing tensions within the revolutionary ranks. Aguinaldo’s character raises questions about loyalty, leadership, and the personal costs of power.

Captain Eduardo Rusca Captain Rusca serves as Luna’s loyal aide-de-camp. He provides both strategic support and emotional grounding for Luna. Rusca’s loyalty and pragmatism stand in contrast to Luna’s fiery temper, offering moments of camaraderie amidst the chaos of war.

Plot

The Struggle for Unity

The film focuses on Heneral Luna’s relentless efforts to unite the fragmented revolutionary forces against American colonizers. Despite his military brilliance, Luna faces resistance not just from foreign enemies but from within his own ranks. Political rivalries, personal ambitions, and regional loyalties undermine his calls for discipline and national unity.

The Rise and Fall

As Luna’s influence grows, so do the threats against him. His confrontational leadership style alienates key political figures, leading to betrayal and conspiracies. The film builds toward a tragic climax, where Luna’s unwavering ideals clash fatally with the harsh realities of politics and power.

The Aftermath

Luna’s assassination becomes a pivotal moment in the revolution, symbolizing the internal divisions that ultimately weakened the fight for independence. The film ends on a somber note, reflecting on the cost of betrayal and the enduring struggle for true freedom.

Setting

Revolutionary Battlefields The film features gritty, realistic battle scenes that showcase the harsh conditions faced by Filipino soldiers. Trenches, makeshift camps, and war-torn landscapes depict the brutal realities of the Philippine-American War. Political Halls and Camps The contrasting settings of Aguinaldo’s political headquarters and Luna’s military camps emphasize the divide between the government’s political maneuvering and the soldiers’ struggles on the front lines. The opulence of government halls starkly contrasts with the chaos of the battlefield.
